# Growth Glossary

> Key terms every growth marketer should know. Concise, practical definitions with real examples.

---

## A

**AARRR (Pirate Metrics)**

*coined by Dave McClure*

**Activation**
The moment a user experiences the core value of your product (the "Aha!" moment).
*Example: Slack â€” sending your first message.*

**Acquisition**
The process of getting users to discover and try your product.
*Channels: SEO, paid ads, content marketing, referrals, partnerships.*

**A/B Testing**
Comparing two versions (A vs B) to see which performs better on a specific metric.

**Aha Moment**
The key activation point where users realize why your product is valuable.
*Example: Facebook â€” 7 friends in 10 days.*

---

## B

**B2B (Business-to-Business)**
Selling products/services to other businesses.

**B2C (Business-to-Consumer)**
Selling products/services directly to consumers.

**Bounce Rate**
% of visitors who leave without taking any action.

**Bundling**
Combining multiple products/services into one offer at a discount.

---

## C

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
Total cost to acquire one paying customer.
`CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Cost / New Customers Acquired`

**Churn**
% of customers who stop using your product in a given period.
*Monthly churn = Users lost in month / Users at start of month*

**Conversion Rate**
% of users who complete a desired action.
*Example: 5% of visitors purchase = 5% conversion rate.*

**Cohort Analysis**
Tracking behavior of a group of users who started at the same time.
*Example: "What % of March cohorts are still active in April?"*

**CPC (Cost Per Click)**
Price paid per click in paid advertising.

**CPI / CPA (Cost Per Install / Cost Per Acquisition)**
Price paid per app install or per user acquisition.

**CTR (Click-Through Rate)**
% of people who click on a link or CTA after seeing it.

**CLV / LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)**
Total revenue a customer generates over their entire relationship with your product.
`LTV = Average Order Value Ã— Purchase Frequency Ã— Customer Lifespan`

---

## D

**DAU (Daily Active Users)**
Unique users who engage with your product in a day.

**DAU/MAU Ratio (Stickiness)**
A measure of daily engagement.
*(Higher = stickier product. Good: 50%+. Great: 80%+)*

**Drop-off**
Where users leave your funnel. The gap between each stage.

**D2C (Direct-to-Consumer)**
Selling directly to customers without intermediaries.

---

## E

**Engagement**
How actively users interact with your product.

**Evergreen Content**
Content that stays relevant long after publication.

**Experimentation**
Systematic testing to improve growth metrics.

---

## F

**First Click / First Purchase**
The first conversion after acquisition â€” critical for activation.

**Free Trial**
Letting users try your product before paying.

**Freemium**
Free basic version + paid premium version.

**Frequency**
How often users engage per time period.

---

## G

**Gamification**
Applying game mechanics (points, badges, leaderboards) to non-game contexts.

**Growth Engine**
The combination of channels, loops, and processes that drives scalable growth.

**Growth Hacking**
Creative, low-cost strategies to acquire and retain users.
*(term coined by Sean Ellis)*

**Growth Loop**
A self-reinforcing cycle where output feeds input.
*Example: Viral loop â€” user invites â†’ new user â†’ invites more.*

---

## H

**Habit**
A behavior done automatically with minimal conscious thought.

**Hook Model**
Nir Eyal's 4-step framework: Trigger â†’ Action â†’ Variable Reward â†’ Investment.

**Heatmap**
Visual representation of where users click, scroll, or hover on a page.

---

## I

**ICE Score**
Prioritization framework: Impact Ã— Confidence Ã— Ease (each 1-10).

**Impressions**
Number of times an ad or content is displayed.

**Incentive**
A reward designed to encourage a specific behavior.

**Intent**
What a user is trying to achieve.

---

## J

**JTBD (Jobs to Be Done)**
Framework that asks: "What job did the user hire your product to do?"
*coined by Clayton Christensen*

---

## K

**K-Factor (Viral Coefficient)**
Number of new users each existing user brings in.
*K > 1 = viral growth. K < 1 = needs paid acquisition.*
`K = Invites Sent Ã— Conversion Rate of Invites`

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
A measurable value that shows how effectively you're achieving business objectives.

---

## L

**Landing Page**
A standalone page designed for a specific conversion goal.

**Leakage**
Users dropping out at different funnel stages. Focus on plugging the biggest leak.

**LTV / CAC Ratio**
How much value each customer brings vs how much it cost to get them.
*Healthy: 3:1+. Great: 5:1+.*

**Loyalty Program**
System that rewards repeat engagement/purchases.

---

## M

**MAU (Monthly Active Users)**
Unique users who engage with your product in a month.

**Marketplace**
A platform connecting buyers and sellers. (Uber, Airbnb, eBay, Fiverr)

**Momentum**
Accelerating growth â€” often driven by compounding network effects.

**MoM (Month-over-Month)**
Comparing this month's metric to last month's.

---

## N

**Network Effects**
A product becomes more valuable as more people use it.
*Direct: More users = more value (WhatsApp). Indirect: More supply = more value (Uber).*

**North Star Metric**
A single metric that captures the core value your product delivers.
*coined by Sean Ellis*

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)**
Measures customer loyalty on a 0-10 scale.
*Promoters (9-10) - Detractors (0-6) = NPS*

---

## O

**Onboarding**
The process of guiding a new user to their first "Aha!" moment.

**Omnichannel**
Consistent experience across all channels (app, web, physical, SMS, email).

**Organic Growth**
Growth from non-paid sources (word-of-mouth, SEO, virality).

---

## P

**Payback Period**
Time needed to earn back the CAC from a customer.
*CAC = 300k, Monthly Revenue = 50k â†’ Payback = 6 months*

**Personalization**
Tailoring the user experience based on individual behavior, preferences, or data.

**PLG (Product-Led Growth)**
Growth driven by the product itself â€” not sales or marketing.
*Example: Slack, Canva, Notion, Zoom*

**Power Users**
Your most engaged, most valuable user segment.

**PR (Public Relations)**
Managing public perception and media coverage.

**Push Notification**
A message sent to a user's device outside of app usage.

---

## R

**Reactivation**
Bringing lapsed or churned users back.

**Referral**
Encouraging existing users to bring new users.

**Retention**
Keeping users engaged over time.

**Retargeting**
Showing ads to users who previously visited but didn't convert.

**Revenue**
Money generated from users/customers.

**ROI (Return on Investment)**
`(Gain from Investment - Cost of Investment) / Cost of Investment`

**RICE Score**
Prioritization: Reach Ã— Impact Ã— Confidence Ã— Effort (effort inverted).

---

## S

**Segmentation**
Dividing users into groups based on behavior, demographics, or attributes.

**SEO (Search Engine Optimization)**
Improving organic search visibility.

**SaaS (Software as a Service)**
Subscription-based software delivery model.

**SMS Marketing**
Marketing via text messages. High open rates, lower volume.

**Stickiness**
How often users return. Measured as DAU/MAU.

**SMART Goals**
Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.

---

## T

**Triggers**
Cues that prompt user action. External (notification, email) or Internal (emotion, habit).

**Time to Value (TTV)**
How long it takes a new user to experience core value.

**TAM / SAM / SOM**
- TAM: Total Addressable Market (everyone)
- SAM: Serviceable Available Market (you can reach)
- SOM: Serviceable Obtainable Market (you can win)

---

## U

**UGC (User-Generated Content)**
Content created by users â€” reviews, templates, posts, videos.

**Unit Economics**
Revenue and cost per individual customer.

**UPI (Unique Point of Interaction)**
A single, measurable touchpoint between user and product.

---

## V

**Viral Coefficient (K-Factor)**
Users brought in per existing user. K > 1 = viral.

**Viral Loop**
A growth loop driven by user-to-user sharing.

**Virality**
The tendency of a product/message to spread rapidly.

**Volume**
The number of users or transactions at a given stage.

---

## W

**WAU (Weekly Active Users)**
Unique users who engage in a week.

**Word-of-Mouth (WoM)**
Organic recommendation from one person to another.

**WOW (Week-over-Week)**
Comparing this week's metric to last week's.

---

## Z

**Zero-Party Data**
Data users intentionally and proactively share with a brand.
*Example: Preferences, purchase intentions, personal context.*
