## June 2026 Trends

### 1. AI Agents Are Eating Growth Operations
- **Why now:** 2026 is the year AI agents moved from demo to production. Growth teams are deploying autonomous agents that segment users, design offers, write copy, schedule campaigns, and analyze results — all without human touchpoints.
- **Read:** [Brian Balfour: AI × Growth Stack 2026](https://brianbalfour.com/)
- **Action:** Map one campaign flow today. How many steps can an AI agent own end-to-end? Start with campaign monitoring (takes 30 min to set up with n8n).

### 2. Zero-Engagement Retention
- **Why now:** Users are saturated with push notifications, emails, and in-app messages. The winning growth teams are building retention *without* engaging the user — through automatic top-ups, passive streak mechanics, and ambient rewards that work in the background.
- **Read:** Duolingo's passive streak feature — users don't even need to open the app
- **Action:** Audit your retention mechanics. Which ones require user action? Can any be made passive?

### 3. Super-App Creep
- **Why now:** Grab, Shopee, and Zalo are all eating each other's lunch. The boundary between "payments app," "e-commerce app," and "chat app" is gone. Growth is now about *ecosystem lock-in* — the more services a user consumes inside your app, the harder to leave.
- **Read:** [Andrew Chen: Super-App strategies](https://andrewchen.com/)
- **Action:** What adjacent needs do your most loyal users have? Can you fulfill one without building a new product? (Partnership > Build)

### 4. AI Personalization at Scale — Beyond "Hi [Name]"
- **Why now:** Real personalization (dynamic offer, dynamic timing, dynamic channel per user) used to require expensive ML teams. Now it's a prompt in n8n with ChatGPT. Personalization is no longer a competitive moat — it's table stakes.
- **Read:** [Lenny: Personalization Playbook](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/)
- **Action:** Run your last campaign through an AI personalization prompt (folder 09). Compare the AI-generated segment-offer map with what you actually did. The gap is your opportunity.

### 5. Video-First Growth Loops
- **Why now:** TikTok, Reels, Shorts have rewired user expectations. Growth loops that used to be "share → invite → reward" are now "create → share → virality → install." User-generated content is the cheapest acquisition channel in 2026.
- **Read:** [Reforge: Viral Loops 2026](https://www.reforge.com/)
- **Action:** Can your product generate a 15-second shareable video? If not, how can you add one UGC moment to the user journey?

### 6. Privacy-First Growth Stack
- **Why now:** Third-party cookies are fully deprecated. Apple ATT is standard. Google's Privacy Sandbox is live. The old playbook (track everywhere → retarget) is dead. Growth teams are rebuilding on first-party data, zero-party data, and contextual targeting.
- **Read:** [Kevin Indig: Privacy & Growth](https://growthunhinged.com/)
- **Action:** What can you ask users directly (zero-party) instead of tracking? Start with a preferences center during onboarding.

### 7. n8n & No-Code Automation Becomes Default
- **Why now:** Growth teams at companies of all sizes are standardizing on n8n (open source) as their automation layer — replacing expensive martech stacks with a single visual tool + AI. Make.com and Zapier are alternatives but n8n wins on cost and customizability.
- **Read:** [n8n Growth Workflows (this repo → 10-n8n-growth-workflows)](./10-n8n-growth-workflows/)
- **Action:** Pick one manual reporting task. Automate it with n8n this week. The 5 workflows in this repo are ready to import.
