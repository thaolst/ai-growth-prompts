## 2026 Trends

### 1. Retention is the New Growth
- **Why now:** CAC is rising everywhere, making acquisition-only strategies unsustainable. Growth teams are shifting focus from "more users" to "longer users."
- **Read:** [Lenny: Retention 101](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/retention-101)
- **Action:** Audit your own retention curves. Where's the drop-off? Focus there.

### 2. AI-Native Growth Teams
- **Why now:** AI tools are moving from "nice to have" to "core infrastructure" for growth teams â€” from personalized push notifications to dynamic pricing engines.
- **Follow:** Brian Balfour's take on [AI Ã— Growth](https://brianbalfour.com/)
- **Action:** Map your growth stack. Identify 3 manual tasks AI can automate today.

### 3. Super-App Thinking
- **Why now:** Platforms combining payments, lending, e-commerce, and ride-hailing are cross-selling between verticals.
- **Read:** Andrew Chen on [Super-App strategies](https://andrewchen.com/)
- **Action:** What adjacent services could your product offer to increase stickiness?

### 4. Compounding Network Effects
- **Why now:** Winner-take-most dynamics are accelerating. Growth teams need to identify and amplify network effects.
- **Read:** [The Cold Start Problem](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/53286719-the-cold-start-problem) by Andrew Chen
- **Action:** Identify your core network effect. Measure its strength quarterly.

### 5. Gamification 2.0
- **Why now:** Basic badges and points are dead. Modern gamification uses behavioral economics, loss aversion, and commitment loops.
- **Read:** Duolingo's growth team [interview](https://www.lennyspodcast.com/)
- **Action:** Map your user journey to find "boredom points" where engagement drops.
