# Growth Frameworks & Mental Models

## Tiếng Việt

Các mental model growth cốt lõi - AARRR, North Star, Growth Loops, Hook Model. Học một lần, dùng mãi mãi.

---

## English

The mental models that power every great growth strategy. Learn them once, apply them everywhere.

---

## 1. AARRR (Pirate Metrics)

**Creator:** Dave McClure / 500 Startups

The classic growth funnel - every growth strategy maps to at least one of these stages.

```
Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral
```

- **Acquisition:** How do users find you? (SEO, paid, viral, content, partnerships)
- **Activation:** When do users experience the "Aha!" moment?
- **Retention:** Do users come back after day 1, week 1, month 1?
- **Revenue:** Do they pay? How much? How often?
- **Referral:** Do they bring others?

**When to use:** Always. Map every experiment to a specific AARRR stage.

---

## 2. North Star Metric

**Creator:** Sean Ellis

The single metric that best captures the core value your product delivers to customers. It aligns every team toward the same outcome.

**Examples:**
- **Spotify:** Time spent listening
- **Airbnb:** Nights booked
- **Facebook:** Daily active users
- **Slack:** Messages sent

**When to use:** When your team is optimizing for multiple conflicting metrics and needs alignment.

---

## 3. Growth Loops

**Creator:** Brian Balfour / Reforge

Instead of a linear funnel (AARRR), growth loops are closed systems where the output feeds back into the input.

**4 Types:**

- **Viral Loop:** User → Invite → New User → Invite...
  - *Dropbox:* Referrals unlocked storage
- **Content Loop:** Create Content → Get Traffic → Convert Users → Create More Content...
  - *HubSpot:* Blog → Sign up → More content
- **Paid Loop:** Spend → Acquire → Convert → Earn → Spend More...
  - *Uber:* Acquisition → Trip → Revenue → Re-acquire
- **Sales Loop:** Outreach → Demo → Close → Referral → More Outreach...
  - *Salesforce:* Referral programs for enterprise

**When to use:** When you want compounding, not linear, growth.

## 4. Hook Model

**Creator:** Nir Eyal

A four-step process that builds habit-forming products.

```
Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment
```

- **Trigger:** External (notification) or Internal (boredom)
- **Action:** Simplest behavior (tap, scroll, type)
- **Variable Reward:** Unpredictable payoff (what's in my feed?)
- **Investment:** User puts something in (data, content, time) → increases likelihood of returning

**When to use:** Designing retention loops and habit-forming features.

---

## 5. ICE / RICE Prioritization

**Creators:** Sean Ellis (ICE) / Intercom (RICE)

Score each growth experiment to decide what to run first.

**ICE Score:**
```
Impact × Confidence × Ease
```
(Each scored 1-10)

**RICE Score:**
```
Reach × Impact × Confidence × Effort
```
(Effort in person-weeks, inverted)

**When to use:** Any time you have more ideas than capacity.

---

## 6. Flywheel Effect

**Creator:** Jim Collins (adapted for growth)

Energy stored in one rotation powers the next. Instead of pushing harder, reduce friction in your growth system.

**Example (Amazon):**
Better selection → More customers → More sellers → Better selection

**When to use:** Understanding why your growth is accelerating or decelerating.

---

## 7. Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

**Creator:** Clayton Christensen

People don't buy products; they hire them to do a job.

**Questions to ask:**
- What job did the user "hire" your product for?
- What's the "competing product" they would use otherwise?
- When do they "fire" your product?

**When to use:** Product decisions, messaging, and activation design.

---

## 8. Hook → Habit → Loyalty

**Combining models:**
- Hook gets them in (Hook Model)
- Habit keeps them coming (Retention loops)
- Loyalty makes them advocates (NPS, referral)
